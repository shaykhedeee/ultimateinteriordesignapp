# 21 — Vastu and Indian Interiors Knowledge Base

## 1. Purpose

This document strengthens the app’s India-specific design intelligence with:
- Vastu-aware room logic
- Indian modular interior realities
- client preference translation into system rules

Important note:
Vastu is a traditional design and cultural preference framework, not a building code or scientific guarantee. In the app, it must be modeled as **client-selectable guidance and constraint logic**, not universal truth.

---

## 2. Fact-Checked Vastu Guidance Patterns Found in Major Indian Interior Content

The following patterns are commonly repeated across organized Indian interior brands and design content:

- pooja / mandir is commonly preferred in the **north-east**, with north/east often treated as alternatives.[1](https://www.homelane.com/design-ideas/hacks-and-ideas/mandir-direction-as-per-vastu-in-home/) [2](https://www.designcafe.com/blog/home-interiors/vastu-tips-pooja-room/) [3](https://www.livspace.com/in/magazine/pooja-home-temple)
- kitchen is commonly preferred in the **south-east**, with **north-west** often cited as the secondary acceptable option.[4](https://www.homelane.com/design-ideas/modular-kitchen-design/kitchen-vastu-tips-for-happy-homes/) [5](https://www.designcafe.com/blog/home-interiors/basic-vastu-tips-for-home/) [6](https://www.livspace.com/in/magazine/basic-vastu-home)
- master bedroom is commonly preferred in the **south-west** in Vastu-guided layouts.[5](https://www.designcafe.com/blog/home-interiors/basic-vastu-tips-for-home/) [6](https://www.livspace.com/in/magazine/basic-vastu-home)
- mirrors facing the bed are commonly discouraged in Vastu-oriented bedroom advice.[5](https://www.designcafe.com/blog/home-interiors/basic-vastu-tips-for-home/) [7](https://www.homelane.com/design-ideas/wardrobe-position-vastu-cupboard-facing-direction-almirah-direction)
- pooja should generally avoid adjacency to bathrooms or toilets, and sacred spaces are often recommended away from heavy-traffic or impure zones.[1](https://www.homelane.com/design-ideas/hacks-and-ideas/mandir-direction-as-per-vastu-in-home/) [2](https://www.designcafe.com/blog/home-interiors/vastu-tips-pooja-room/)
- in compact homes, brands often explicitly mention compromise rules such as integrating a pooja unit into a kitchen or bedroom only when required, with directional and respect-oriented safeguards.[2](https://www.designcafe.com/blog/home-interiors/vastu-tips-pooja-room/) [8](https://www.livspace.com/in/magazine/pooja-room-design-latest-by-livspace)

## 3. How the App Should Model Vastu

## 3.1 Vastu Mode Levels
The app should support three modes:

### Mode A — Off
No Vastu evaluation.

### Mode B — Advisory
Suggest better placements and flag conflicts, but do not block layouts.

### Mode C — Strict Preference
Apply stronger ranking penalties and flag hard conflicts where the client explicitly wants strong adherence.

## 4. Vastu Rule Categories

### Entrance
- clutter-free entrance preferred
- well-lit entrance preferred
- door opening direction may be a client preference flag

### Pooja / Mandir
- north-east preferred
- east/north acceptable alternatives
- avoid toilet adjacency where possible
- avoid placement directly under staircases where client marks strict Vastu preference
- prefer calm colours and respectful separation from noisy zones

### Kitchen
- south-east preferred
- north-west fallback often accepted
- cooking direction preference may be east-facing in some client expectations
- avoid direct toilet adjacency where possible
- stove and sink separation may be treated as a design and Vastu convenience rule

### Bedroom
- master bedroom south-west preferred
- guest/children bedroom north-west often acceptable
- avoid mirrors facing bed in Vastu-sensitive mode

### Furniture / Decor
- heavy furniture often preferred on south/west walls in many Vastu-oriented layouts
- clutter and blocked entrances should affect Vastu score

## 5. Indian Interior Design Knowledge Beyond Vastu

A great Indian interior app must understand more than Vastu.

## 5.1 Typical Indian Residential Needs
- heavy storage requirements
- modular kitchen priority
- wardrobe/loft integration
- pooja corner or dedicated mandir need
- utility/storage for cleaning supplies and appliances
- family seating and TV wall importance
- mixed formal/informal living use
- guest accommodation flexibility
- climate-responsive materials

## 5.2 Common Indian Material Decision Factors
- termite/moisture resistance
- ease of cleaning
- hardware longevity
- dust visibility
- fingerprint resistance
- stain resistance in kitchen and pooja areas
- ability to handle haldi/kumkum/oil in pooja spaces

## 5.3 City / Climate Sensitivity
Examples from organized interiors content frequently mention climate and maintenance considerations for materials in different Indian cities, such as humidity or dust resistance.[9](https://www.homelane.com/cities/interior-designers-bhubaneswar) [10](https://www.homelane.com/cities/interior-designers-hyderabad)

The app should therefore support:
- climate profile tags per project city
- material suitability scoring by climate
- maintenance preference filters

## 6. Product Rules for Vastu and Indian Interiors

1. Vastu must be optional and configurable.
2. Vastu rules must be explainable to users.
3. Budget must not be ignored when applying Vastu-sensitive design.
4. The app should suggest practical compromise layouts when perfect adherence is not geometrically possible.
5. Indian room-specific priorities must influence templates and suggestions.

---

## 7. Final Knowledge Rule

> The app must treat Vastu as one important layer of Indian interior decision-making, but not the only one. It should combine Vastu guidance, modular practicality, maintenance logic, budget fit, and production reality into a coherent Indian design intelligence system.
