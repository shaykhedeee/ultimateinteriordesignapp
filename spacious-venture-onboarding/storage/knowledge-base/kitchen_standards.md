# Modular Kitchen Design & Architectural Standards

This document serves as the official reference standard for Spacious Venture Modular Kitchen designs in contemporary Indian residences. All visualizers and design agents must respect these guidelines to ensure structural realism and functional correctness.

---

## 📐 Sizing & Elevation Matrix

Standard heights and depths for Indian modular cabinetry are crucial to ensure modular drawers and standard appliances fit seamlessly:

| Cabinet Tier | Height (mm) | Depth (mm) | Description / Placement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Units** | 850 | 560 - 600 | Bottom cabinets supporting countertops, hobs, and sinks. Fits standard tandem drawers. |
| **Countertop** | 20 - 40 | 600 - 620 | Solid quartz, granite, or marble slab. |
| **Backsplash (Dado)** | 600 | — | Wall tiles or stone cladding between countertop and wall cabinets. |
| **Wall Cabinets** | 600 | 300 - 350 | Lower tier upper cabinets. Houses chimney vents, plate racks, and glass display. |
| **Top Lofts** | 600 - 900 | 560 - 600 | Uppermost cabinet tier touching the ceiling. Used for heavy storage. |

## 🎨 Color Separation & Laminate Coding

A common mistake in interior rendering is color bleeding between cabinet tiers. When contrasting laminate sheets are requested, follow these strict boundaries:

1. **Top Lofts**: The uppermost storage units touching the ceiling. Often requested in a contrasting neutral finish (e.g., beige or grey matte laminate).
2. **Wall Cabinets**: The cabinets situated directly below the lofts. Often kept in a light reflective finish (e.g., pure white glossy acrylic or matte laminate) to keep the prep area bright.
3. **Base Units**: The lower cabinets beneath the countertop. Often match the lofts or act as a primary color anchor (e.g., grey or beige).
4. **Boundary Separation**: Always render a clean, distinct horizontal gap or shadow line between the beige top lofts and the white wall cabinets. Never let the loft color bleed into the wall cabinets.

---

## 🍳 Appliance & Fixture Layout Coordinates

1. **Hob & Cooktop**: Place a sleek black multi-burner gas hob. It requires a high-suction chimney mounted on the wall directly above it.
2. **Sink**: The kitchen sink should be an under-counter single/double bowl mount. It is typically positioned directly beneath a window to maximize natural light and ventilation.
3. **Appliance Swap**: When the user requests a swap, the gas hob moves to the left wall (with the chimney above it), and the sink moves to the right wall (placed under the window).
4. **Loft Alignment**: Upper lofts on the left wall can terminate early, aligning precisely with the edge of the window frame or where the sink counter begins. This leaves the window zone open and prevents blockages.
5. **Uniform Loft Height**: Ensure that all top lofts across both walls maintain a uniform height and straight aligned door lines, creating a clean horizontal ceiling border.
