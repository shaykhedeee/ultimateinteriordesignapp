# 📖 Comprehensive Interior Design Lingo & Material Dictionary

This document serves as the vocabulary reference for Spacious Venture's design models. It explains key terminology, material types, and visual concepts to ensure prompt accuracy and correct design output.

---

## 🪵 Cabinetry & Wood Work Lingo

1. **Fluted Paneling (Rafters)**:
   - Repeated vertical wood slats or half-round dowel moldings that create a ribbed, linear texture.
   - Used for feature walls, TV unit backdrops, or to wrap around door panels.
2. **Concealed Flush Door (Hidden Door)**:
   - A door leaf that is mounted on concealed 3D hinges, sitting perfectly flush with the wall panel.
   - When covered in vertical fluted rafters, the door joints disappear, making the door completely invisible.
3. **Groove Profile (Gola Profile / Finger Pull)**:
   - A continuous metal profile (J-type or C-type) integrated into the cabinet carcass.
   - Enables handleless door operation by providing a recessed channel for fingers to pull the drawers.
4. **Tandem Box Drawers**:
   - High-end double-walled metal drawer systems with soft-close runners, used for heavy kitchen utensils.
5. **Dado (Backsplash)**:
   - The vertical wall space between the kitchen countertop and upper wall cabinets, usually tiled or clad in marble.

## 🪨 Stone & Surface Materials

1. **Book-Matched Statutario Marble**:
   - A high-end white Italian marble with bold, dramatic grey veins.
   - "Book-matched" means adjacent slabs are polished on opposite sides so that their veining patterns mirror each other like an open book, creating a symmetrical design.
2. **Waterfall Edge Countertop**:
   - A countertop design where the stone slab extends vertically down the side of the cabinetry or kitchen island all the way to the floor, creating a continuous stone wrap.
3. **Quartz Surface**:
   - An engineered stone made of 93% quartz crystals and resin. Offers high stain resistance and uniform pattern. Used as an alternative to marble.

## 💡 Lighting & Ambience Lingo

1. **Cove Lighting**:
   - Indirect lighting built into ledges, recesses, or high valances. The light source is hidden and reflects off the ceiling or wall to cast a soft, diffused glow.
2. **Backlit Panel**:
   - Strip LEDs mounted behind a decorative panel (e.g. marble or wood) facing the wall, creating a glowing halo effect around the panel edges.
3. **Task Spotlights**:
   - Directed downlights (usually 3000K warm white) placed directly over working areas like kitchen countertops or sink basins.
4. **Profile LED Strips**:
   - Slim aluminum profiles housing LED strips with diffuser covers, routed flush into cabinet bottoms or wardrobe shelves.
