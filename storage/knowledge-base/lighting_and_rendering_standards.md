# 💡 Lighting & Rendering Standards

This document establishes the lighting, perspective, and rendering standards for Spacious Venture 3D visualizers to ensure high-fidelity CGI output.

---

## ☀️ Lighting Hierarchy

To achieve a photorealistic, premium feel, renders must balance three distinct light sources:

1. **Natural Ambient Daylight**:
   - Primary soft light entering from large floor-to-ceiling windows or balcony openings.
   - Casts long, soft shadows and highlights material textures naturally.
2. **Warm Ambient LED Backlighting (3000K)**:
   - Hidden strip lights behind TV backdrops, under loft cabinets, and in ceiling coves.
   - Adds depth and warmth to the interior composition.
3. **Directed Task Spotlights (4000K)**:
   - Recessed ceiling fixtures pointing at workstations (countertop, dining surface).
   - High color rendering index (CRI) to display wood and stone colors accurately.

---

## 📷 Camera Perspectives & Frames

1. **Diagonal Perspective**:
   - Taken from a corner angle at a height of **1200 mm - 1500 mm** (seated eye level).
   - Showcases structural depth, floor plan flow, and multiple walls.
2. **Elevation View**:
   - Straight-on camera perpendicular to the feature wall.
   - Mandatory for detailed cabinetry reviews, showing alignment, horizontal lines, and door divisions.
3. **No Distortion Rules**:
   - Camera lens must maintain a standard wide angle without fisheye distortion.
   - Vertical lines must remain straight and perpendicular to the floor.

---

## 🎨 Materials Representation

- **Glossy Laminate / Acrylic**: Highly reflective surfaces. Reflection must show soft blurs of windows and lights.
- **Matte Cabinetry**: Soft, light-diffusing finishes. Low reflectivity.
- **Marble Veining**: veining must match on adjacent panels (book-matching). Veneer grain must run vertically unless specified otherwise.
